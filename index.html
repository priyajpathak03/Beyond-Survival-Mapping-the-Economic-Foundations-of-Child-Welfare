Beyond Survival: Mapping the Economic Foundations of Child Welfare

A Comprehensive Analysis of UNICEF Data (2010-2023)

Executive Summary

This report examines the relationship between national economic prosperity and child health outcomes. Key findings reveal a critical GDP per capita threshold of ~$15,000 beyond which child mortality improvements plateau.

Analysis Insights

  • Global Disparity: The wealthiest quintile experiences significantly lower mortality than the poorest.
  • The $15,000 Threshold: Diminishing returns on child survival are observed once GDP exceeds this level.
  • Temporal Gains: Global life expectancy has shown steady gains despite regional setbacks.
